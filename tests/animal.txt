===Etymology 1===
From {{inh|en|enm|animal}}, from {{der|en|fro|animal}}, from {{der|en|la|animal}}, a nominal use of an adjective from {{m|la|animale}}, neuter of {{m|la|animālis}}, from {{m|la|anima|t=breath, spirit}}. Displaced native Middle English {{m|enm|deor|deor, der|animal}} (from {{cog|ang|dēor|t=animal}}), {{cog|enm|reother|t=animal, neat}} (from {{cog|ang|hriþer|hrīþer, hrȳþer|t=neat, ox}}).

====Noun====
{{en-noun}}

# In {{l|en|scientific}} usage, a [[multicellular]] [[organism]] that is usually {{l|en|mobile}}, whose {{l|en|cell}}s are not encased in a rigid [[cell wall]] (distinguishing it from {{l|en|plant|plants}} and {{l|en|fungi}}) and which derives energy solely from the consumption of other [[organism]]s (distinguishing it from {{l|en|plant|plants}}).
#: {{ux|en|A cat is an '''animal''', not a plant. Humans are also '''animals''', under the scientific definition, as we are not plants.}}
